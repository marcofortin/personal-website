Garden of Love

This delicate garden of flower,
Offered to us, men
And always to be desired.

We carelessly ran and ran,
Disturbing the innocent peace
Carried by this blossom

Leaving for war,
We neglected this heaven
And came back to a fallen nation.

Those flowers,
they had always been perceived as object of affection
And never as a gift to open.

We seek in our selfishness a reason,
A logical explanation
To our false domination.

However, we we’re too foolish to realise,
That this gift offered was for us to cherish.
Not as a property, more as an ally.

I hope for us a better tomorrow,
A future in which we acknowledge in time
The authentic power of femininity.

Let’s allow nature’s gift to flourish,
Let’s stop hampering their growth
And let’s appreciate their richness.

I speak for men what I say I’m sorry.
Let’s not forget our past,
But perhaps build on it towards equity.

Marc-Olivier Fortin
